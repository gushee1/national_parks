Our Impact

National Parks 101 aims to immerse players in a virtual environment similar to those of the National Parks. While playing, players will encounter potentially dangerous wildlife commonly experienced by hikers exploring these parks. Our goal is that by playing our game, players can become prepared to handle the risks that come with hiking in the National Parks in a safe environment. We will teach the player how to handle dangerous wildlife encounters, so they will be prepared if it happened in real life. In addition to user safety, our game will educate users on various wildlife and survival tips, as well as teach them how to enjoy national parks in a respectful way.

Novelty

Existing games, such as Green Hell VR and The Forest, use virtual reality to create an immersive survival experince for the player. Similar to National Parks 101, they allow the player to experience different dangerous situations, forcing the player to react to survive. However, unlike other wilderness survival based virtual reality games, National Parks 101 seeks to provide the player an experience that will prepare them for real world situations rather than just a recreational experience. By simulating common realistic scenarios encountered by hikers in National Parks, players will learn more meaningful skills through their interactions in our game than in any other VR survival game. In particular, our game will focus on realistic wildlife interactions, and how to react in the real world to dangerous wildlife encounters.

Financial sustainability

Our first source of income will be funding from from various private, state, and national parks. Our VR experience will include various real life parks that the user can experience virtually. We will partner with parks to include their park inside our game as one of the levels the players can experience. This will increase interest in their parks, while also helping people prepare for species of animals they can expect to encounter at the park. We also plan to provide our VR service and equipment to parks for a monthly subscription fee. Parks can set up our equipment in areas like a visitor center, so park goers can prepare for the experience before their hike. Since we will recreate these parks inside our game, this will also help them identify trails and landmarks to prevent them from getting lost in their actual hike.

In addition to being an educational tool, we aim to make our product enjoyable as a standalone experience. There are over 300 million people who visit national parks every year, so there is a lot of interest in national park related experiences. We aim to make a large portion of sales through individual users off of various VR game marketplaces.
